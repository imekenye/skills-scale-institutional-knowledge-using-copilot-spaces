# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Clarifies requirements, estimates features, provides technical feedback
- **Project Managers**: Reports progress, flags blockers and dependencies
- **Scrum Master**: Participates in ceremonies, escalates impediments
- **Technical Lead**: Seeks guidance on architecture, participates in code reviews
- **UX Designer**: Implements designs, provides feedback on feasibility
- **Business Analyst**: Asks questions about requirements and acceptance criteria

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides requirements, prioritizes backlog, accepts completed work
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on roadmap, scope, and stakeholder expectations
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on backlog refinement and sprint goals
- **Technical Lead**: Discusses technical trade-offs and feasibility
- **UX Designer**: Validates user needs, reviews designs against product vision
- **Business Analyst**: Partners on requirement definition and acceptance criteria

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Tracks progress, manages dependencies, escalates blockers
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on roadmap, scope changes, and priorities
- **Scrum Master**: Coordinates on team capacity, velocity, and process health
- **Technical Lead**: Reviews technical risks, resource needs, and timelines
- **UX Designer**: Manages design milestones and integration points
- **Business Analyst**: Ensures requirements are complete and tracked

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on agile practices. They serve as servant leaders who ensure the team can focus on delivering value.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow down the team
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external disruptions
- Track and communicate team velocity and capacity
- Foster continuous improvement and team self-organization

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Ensure consistent adherence to agile processes
- Create a collaborative, transparent work environment
- Enable team autonomy and accountability

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standup meetings
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Blocker escalation to stakeholders
- Team health and velocity metrics reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Removes blockers, facilitates ceremonies, coaches on practices
- **Product Managers**: Ensures backlog is ready, facilitates prioritization discussions
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on dependencies, shares team capacity and velocity
- **Technical Lead**: Collaborates on technical impediments and process improvements
- **UX Designer**: Ensures design work is integrated into sprint planning
- **Business Analyst**: Coordinates on requirement clarification and acceptance criteria

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive interfaces, and validate solutions through user testing. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Ensure accessibility and design system consistency
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Base design decisions on user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Prototypes and design specifications
- Usability test results and recommendations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides design specs, collaborates on implementation constraints
- **Product Managers**: Validates user needs, translates requirements into designs
- **Project Managers**: Communicates design timelines and dependencies
- **Scrum Master**: Participates in sprint planning with design-ready work
- **Technical Lead**: Discusses technical feasibility and design system architecture
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates on user stories and acceptance criteria

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, architect solutions, and mentor the development team. They ensure code quality, scalability, and alignment with technical standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review and approve major technical decisions
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Identify and resolve technical debt
- Evaluate new technologies and tools
- Ensure security, performance, and scalability standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers on technical trade-offs

### Goals
- Maintain high code quality and architectural integrity
- Enable team technical growth and productivity
- Balance innovation with stability
- Minimize technical risk and debt

### Typical Communication
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical design documents and diagrams
- Code review feedback and pair programming
- Technical roadmap and debt reduction plans

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Mentors, reviews code, guides on technical decisions
- **Product Managers**: Advises on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- **Project Managers**: Estimates technical complexity, identifies risks
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on technical impediments and process improvements
- **UX Designer**: Discusses technical constraints affecting design
- **Business Analyst**: Translates requirements into technical solutions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and delivery teams by gathering requirements, defining acceptance criteria, and ensuring solutions meet business needs.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Define clear acceptance criteria for features
- Analyze and model business processes
- Facilitate requirement workshops and reviews
- Validate solutions against business objectives
- Support UAT (User Acceptance Testing) activities

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Align technical solutions with business goals
- Minimize rework from requirement ambiguity
- Enable informed business decisions

### Typical Communication
- Requirement documents and user stories
- Acceptance criteria and test scenarios
- Process flow diagrams and data models
- Stakeholder requirement reviews and sign-offs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements, answers questions during implementation
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on requirement prioritization and scope
- **Project Managers**: Provides input on requirement completeness and risks
- **Scrum Master**: Ensures stories are ready for sprint planning
- **UX Designer**: Collaborates on user stories and acceptance criteria
- **Technical Lead**: Validates feasibility and explores technical solutions
